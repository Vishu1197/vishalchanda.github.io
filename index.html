About VirexaAnalytics

VirexaAnalytics was founded to bridge the gap between complex biological data and scientific communication, helping researchers transform their work into clear, structured, and publication-ready manuscripts.

  • Transforming raw experimental and computational data into publishable research
  • Combining bioinformatics expertise with scientific writing
  • Supporting researchers in achieving high-quality journal publications

What I Do

  • Bioinformatics Analysis (RNA-seq, Microarray, Differential Expression)
  • Drug Discovery using Molecular Docking & Molecular Dynamics Simulation
  • ADMET Profiling and Protein–Ligand Interaction Analysis
  • KEGG Pathway & Gene Ontology (GO) Analysis
  • Manuscript Writing (Research & Review Articles)
  • Scientific Editing & Proofreading

Why I Started This

During my research journey, I observed that many researchers generate valuable data but struggle with interpretation, structuring, and presenting their findings effectively. Complex analyses such as RNA-seq, molecular docking, and pathway studies often remain underutilized due to gaps in scientific writing.

VirexaAnalytics was created to bridge this gap—helping researchers not only analyze their data, but also communicate it with clarity, accuracy, and scientific impact.

About Me

I am a PhD scholar in Biotechnology with expertise in bioinformatics, nano-biotechnology, and virology. My research focuses on understanding complex biological systems using computational and experimental approaches.

With hands-on experience in RNA-seq analysis, molecular docking, and scientific writing, I aim to help researchers translate their work into meaningful scientific contributions.
